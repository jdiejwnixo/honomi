How We Grow Honomi Rice

We grow our rice without chemicals, working in harmony with nature and carefully protecting the balance of the ecosystem. Every step of our farming process is guided by our belief that agriculture should preserve the land, not harm it.

Our Rice Process

Plowing

The soil is plowed to loosen the earth, turn over weeds naturally, and create a flat surface for planting.

→

Seedling

Rice seedlings are first grown in small nursery beds for around 30–50 days before being transplanted by hand into the rice fields.

→

Planting

Each seedling is planted one by one by hand at carefully measured points across the field.

→

Irrigation

A shallow layer of water is maintained in the paddies to support healthy rice growth, regulate soil temperature, and naturally suppress weeds.

→

Weeding & Fertilizing

Weeds around the paddies are removed by hand to ensure nutrients go directly to the rice plants. Natural fertilizers are applied to support healthy growth.

→

Pest Control

Instead of using chemicals, Pak Putu creates handmade tools and works alongside nature, including allowing owls to live near the fields, to gently drive pests away without harming other living creatures.

→

Harvesting

Once the rice plants turn golden and fully mature, the rice is carefully harvested.

→

Drying

After harvesting, the rice is dried either naturally under the sun or with drying machines until it reaches the ideal moisture level to preserve quality and prevent spoilage.

→

Milling

The rice is milled to remove the outer husk while preserving the flavor of each grain.

→

Packaging & Sealing

Each package is carefully filled, sealed, and prepared by hand to ensure freshness and quality before reaching customers.
